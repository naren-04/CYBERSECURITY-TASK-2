PHISHING EMAIL ANALYSIS REPORT

1. Spoofed Sender:

-The sender email "support@paypa1.com" is fake. It uses number 1 instead of letter l.

2. Suspicious Link:

-The link "http://paypal-secure-login.com" is not the official PayPal website.

3. Urgent Language:

-The email creates fear by saying the account will be locked in 24 hours.

4. Generic Greeting:

-It says "Dear Customer" instead of using a real name.

5. Threatening Message:

-It threatens account suspension to force action.

Conclusion:

-This email is a phishing attempt designed to steal user login information.
